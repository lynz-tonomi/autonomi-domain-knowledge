# Three-Way Matching for F&B Procurement

## Overview

Three-way matching is the cornerstone of AP internal controls for food and beverage companies. It validates that what was ordered (Purchase Order), what was received (Goods Received Note / Receiving Report), and what the vendor is billing (Vendor Invoice) are in agreement before payment is authorized. In F&B, matching is complicated by commodity price volatility, agricultural supply variability, quality-related partial rejections, and the need for lot-level traceability.

---

## 1. The Three Documents

### Purchase Order (PO)
**Source**: Created in QBO or ERP by procurement team upon approved requisition.

**Key fields for matching**:
- PO number (unique identifier)
- Vendor ID and name
- Line items: ingredient/packaging item code, description, UOM (unit of measure)
- Ordered quantity per line
- Agreed unit price per line
- Delivery date (requested)
- Delivery location (warehouse address)
- Payment terms
- Special instructions (organic certification required, temperature requirements, lot size requirements)

**F&B-specific PO considerations**:
- **Commodity pricing**: PO may reference a pricing formula (e.g., "USDA organic cane sugar index + $0.03/lb") rather than a fixed price. Final price determined at delivery.
- **Seasonal availability**: PO may specify acceptable substitutions (e.g., "Brazilian orange juice concentrate or Mexican, 65 Brix minimum")
- **Shelf life requirements**: PO specifies minimum remaining shelf life at delivery (e.g., "minimum 12 months remaining")
- **Certification requirements**: PO specifies required certifications (organic, Non-GMO, kosher, allergen-free)

### Goods Received Note (GRN) / Receiving Report
**Source**: Generated by warehouse team upon physical receipt and inspection of goods.

**Key fields for matching**:
- GRN number
- PO reference number
- Vendor ID
- Actual delivery date and time
- Line items received: item code, description, quantity received, UOM
- Lot numbers assigned
- Condition at receipt (acceptable, damaged, temperature excursion)
- QC status (released, on hold pending testing, rejected)

**F&B-specific GRN requirements**:

| Check | Description | Fail Action |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| **Quantity verification** | Count/weigh received vs. PO quantity | Note shortage or overage on GRN |
| **Temperature check** | Verify cold chain integrity (refrigerated/frozen items) | Reject if temp >41F for refrigerated, >0F for frozen |
| **Visual inspection** | Check for damage, leaks, contamination, pest evidence | Reject damaged units; photograph evidence |
| **COA verification** | Certificate of Analysis from supplier matches specs | Hold until COA received and verified |
| **Allergen declaration** | Verify allergen status matches PO requirements | Reject if allergen cross-contact risk |
| **Lot/date code** | Record lot numbers; verify remaining shelf life | Reject if below minimum shelf life |
| **Organic verification** | Organic certificate matches lot; verify transaction certificate | Hold if organic documentation incomplete |
| **Packaging integrity** | Cases sealed, pallets stable, shrink wrap intact | Note damage; partial reject if warranted |
| **Weight verification** | Net weight matches declared weight (within tolerance) | Short-weight claim if below tolerance |

**QC Hold Process**:
- Items placed on QC hold are received into inventory but segregated (not available for production)
- Micro testing: 24-72 hours for standard panel (APC, yeast/mold, coliforms, pathogens)
- Chemical testing: Brix, pH, titratable acidity, color — typically same day
- QC release: items move from hold status to available; GRN updated with release date
- QC reject: items quarantined; vendor notified; return or destruction coordinated

### Vendor Invoice
**Source**: Received via Bill.com (email ingestion, vendor portal, or manual upload).

**Key fields for matching**:
- Invoice number (vendor's reference)
- PO reference number
- Vendor ID
- Invoice date
- Line items: item description, quantity billed, unit price, extended amount
- Freight charges (if applicable)
- Tax (if applicable)
- Total amount due
- Payment terms and due date

## 2. Matching Rules and Tolerances

### Standard Tolerance Thresholds

| Match Dimension | Tolerance | Notes |
|----------------|-----------|-------|
| **Quantity: PO vs. GRN** | ±5% | Agricultural products may have higher natural variance |
| **Quantity: GRN vs. Invoice** | ±2% | Invoice should closely match what was actually received |
| **Quantity: PO vs. Invoice** | ±5% | Combined tolerance for order-to-bill |
| **Price: PO vs. Invoice** | ±2% | Covers minor price adjustments, rounding |
| **Price: commodity ingredients** | ±10% | For market-priced ingredients with index-based pricing |
| **Total amount** | ±$50 or ±2% (whichever is greater) | Catches rounding and minor discrepancies |
| **Freight charges** | ±5% of quoted freight | Fuel surcharges, accessorial charges may vary |

### Match Categories

**Perfect Match (Green)**
- All three documents agree within tolerances on all dimensions
- Auto-approved for payment (subject to dollar threshold rules)
- No human intervention required

**Minor Exception (Yellow)**
- One or more dimensions outside tolerance but within 2x tolerance
- Routed to AP specialist for review and resolution
- Expected resolution: 1-2 business days
- Common causes: rounding differences, UOM conversion errors, minor overshipment

**Major Exception (Red)**
- One or more dimensions outside 2x tolerance
- Routed to procurement manager and AP manager
- Expected resolution: 3-5 business days
- Common causes: price change not reflected on PO, significant quantity discrepancy, quality rejection

**No Match (Black)**
- Invoice has no matching PO or no matching GRN
- Routed to department manager and CFO
- Expected resolution: 5-10 business days
- Common causes: PO not created, goods not yet received, wrong vendor, fraudulent invoice

### Tolerance by Vendor Category

| Vendor Category | Quantity Tolerance | Price Tolerance | Match Strictness |
|----------------|-------------------|----------------|-----------------|
| Strategic ingredient suppliers | ±5% | ±2% | Strict — full 3-way match required |
| Commodity suppliers (spot buy) | ±10% | ±10% (market-indexed) | Moderate — PO may be blanket |
| Packaging suppliers | ±3% | ±1% | Strict — contract pricing expected |
| Co-packers | ±10% (overrun/underrun) | ±0% (contract rate) | Strict on price; flexible on quantity |
| Freight carriers | N/A | ±5% (quoted vs. actual) | Moderate — fuel surcharge variance normal |
| Service providers | N/A | ±0% | Strict — fixed fee, no quantity variance |
| Spot purchases (<$1,000) | N/A | N/A | PO optional with manager approval |
| Emergency purchases | N/A | N/A | Retroactive PO within 48 hours |

## 3. Exception Handling Workflows

### Price Variance Exception

**Scenario**: Vendor invoices at $0.48/lb for organic cane sugar; PO states $0.45/lb.

**Workflow**:
1. System flags price variance: +6.7% (exceeds ±2% tolerance)
2. Agent checks: is this a market-indexed commodity?
   - Yes → check current market index. If invoice price aligns with index, update PO price and approve.
   - No → route to procurement for vendor negotiation.
3. Procurement reviews:
   - If price increase is legitimate (supplier notification, market conditions): amend PO, approve invoice
   - If price increase is unauthorized: dispute with vendor, hold payment pending resolution
4. If approved: agent updates standard cost in QBO for future variance analysis
5. Journal entry impact: price variance captured in PPV account (5700)

### Quantity Variance Exception

**Scenario**: PO for 10,000 lbs of lemon juice concentrate. GRN shows 9,200 lbs received. Invoice bills for 10,000 lbs.

**Workflow**:
1. System flags: GRN quantity (9,200) vs. Invoice quantity (10,000) = -8% variance
2. Agent checks GRN for notes: was shortage documented at receiving?
   - Shortage noted on GRN: file shortage claim with vendor
   - No shortage noted: investigate — did warehouse miss a pallet? Check all receiving records.
3. Resolution paths:
   - **Confirmed shortage**: Request vendor credit memo for 800 lbs. Pay invoice minus shorted amount.
   - **Receiving error**: Warehouse confirms all product received. Correct GRN quantity to 10,000. Match resolves.
   - **Partial delivery**: Remaining 800 lbs on backorder. Pay for 9,200 lbs now; separate invoice for remainder.
4. Shortage claim tracking: vendor has 15 business days to respond; escalate if no response.

### Quality Hold Exception

**Scenario**: 5,000 lbs of probiotic powder received. QC testing in progress. Vendor invoice arrives.

**Workflow**:
1. System flags: GRN status = "QC Hold" — match incomplete until release
2. Invoice coded but not approved for payment until QC release
3. QC testing outcomes:
   - **Released**: GRN updated to "Released." Three-way match proceeds normally.
   - **Conditional release**: Acceptable for some products but not others. Partial GRN release.
   - **Rejected**: Full rejection. GRN quantity set to zero for matching. Vendor return authorization (RMA) initiated. Invoice disputed.
4. QC hold duration tracking: if hold exceeds 5 business days, escalate to QC manager
5. Rejected material: coordinate return logistics; vendor issues credit memo or replacement

### Overrun/Underrun Exception (Co-Packer)

**Scenario**: PO for 100,000 cans. Co-packer produces 108,000 cans (8% overrun, within ±10% tolerance).

**Workflow**:
1. GRN records 108,000 cans received
2. Invoice bills for 108,000 cans at contract toll rate
3. System checks: overrun within tolerance (±10%)? Yes — 8% within tolerance
4. Auto-adjust PO quantity to 108,000 (or create PO amendment)
5. Additional 8,000 cans represent unplanned inventory — flag for demand planning
6. Approve invoice for full 108,000 cans
7. If overrun exceeds tolerance (e.g., 112,000 = +12%): negotiate with co-packer; may refuse excess

## 4. Document Flow: End-to-End

### Complete Procurement-to-Payment Flow

```
Step 1: REQUISITION
  Procurement identifies need (ingredient running low, production schedule requires material)
  Creates purchase requisition with specs, quantity, required-by date
  ↓
Step 2: SOURCING & PO CREATION
  Procurement selects vendor (approved vendor list, competitive quote if required)
  Creates Purchase Order in QBO with line items, prices, delivery details
  PO sent to vendor via email or EDI
  ↓
Step 3: VENDOR ACKNOWLEDGMENT
  Vendor confirms PO: accepts price, quantity, delivery date
  Any exceptions negotiated (partial availability, price adjustment, alternate delivery date)
  PO amended if needed
  ↓
Step 4: GOODS SHIPMENT
  Vendor ships goods; provides BOL, tracking, and advance ship notice (ASN)
  If international: provides commercial invoice, packing list, COA, phytosanitary certificate
  ↓
Step 5: GOODS RECEIPT & INSPECTION
  Warehouse receives goods; performs receiving inspection per GRN checklist
  Records actual quantities, lot numbers, condition, temperature
  Places items on QC hold if testing required
  Generates GRN in WMS; data available for matching
  ↓
Step 6: QC TESTING & RELEASE (if applicable)
  Lab performs micro, chemical, sensory testing per specification
  Results compared against ingredient specification sheet
  Release or reject decision documented
  GRN updated with QC status
  ↓
Step 7: VENDOR INVOICE RECEIPT
  Invoice arrives in Bill.com (email, portal, or upload)
  OCR/AI extracts invoice data
  Agent matches to PO and GRN
  ↓
Step 8: THREE-WAY MATCH
  System compares: PO quantities/prices vs. GRN quantities vs. Invoice quantities/prices
  Perfect match → auto-approve (within dollar threshold)
  Exception → route to appropriate handler per exception type
  ↓
Step 9: APPROVAL
  Approved by appropriate authority per threshold rules
  Approval recorded with timestamp, approver ID in Bill.com audit trail
  ↓
Step 10: PAYMENT
  Invoice queued for next payment run
  Payment executed via configured method (ACH, wire, virtual card, check)
  Remittance advice sent to vendor
  Payment syncs to QBO; reconciles with bank feed
```

## 5. Integration Architecture

### System Roles

| System | Role in 3-Way Match |
|--------|-------------------|
| **QBO** | Source of truth for PO (creation, amendment, closure). GL coding master. Financial reporting. |
| **WMS (Warehouse Management)** | Source of truth for GRN (receiving, inspection, QC status, lot tracking). |
| **Bill.com** | Source of truth for vendor invoice (intake, coding, approval routing, payment execution). |
| **Autonomi Agent** | Orchestrator — pulls data from all three systems, performs matching logic, routes exceptions, executes approvals. |

### Data Flow for Matching

```
QBO (PO Data) ──────────────┐
                             │
WMS (GRN Data) ─────────────┤──→ Autonomi Agent ──→ Match Result
                             │       │
Bill.com (Invoice Data) ─────┘       │
                                     ├──→ Auto-approve (perfect match, within threshold)
                                     ├──→ Exception queue (mismatch)
                                     └──→ Reject (no PO, fraud risk, duplicate)
```

### API Integration Points

**QBO → Agent** (PO data):
```
GET /v3/company/{realmId}/purchaseorder/{poId}
```
Agent extracts: line items, quantities, prices, vendor reference, status.

**WMS → Agent** (GRN data):
- Webhook on receiving completion event
- Or polling: GET WMS API for receiving records by PO reference
- Agent extracts: received quantities, lot numbers, QC status, condition notes

**Bill.com → Agent** (Invoice data):
- Webhook on new bill creation event
- Or polling: GET Bill.com API for bills in "awaiting approval" status
- Agent extracts: line items, quantities, prices, PO reference, total amount

### Matching Algorithm (Pseudocode)

```
for each new_invoice in bill_com.get_pending_invoices():
    po = qbo.get_purchase_order(new_invoice.po_reference)
    if not po:
        flag_as_no_match(new_invoice, reason="No PO found")
        continue

    grn = wms.get_receiving_report(po.po_number)
    if not grn:
        flag_as_incomplete(new_invoice, reason="Goods not yet received")
        continue

    if grn.qc_status == "HOLD":
        flag_as_qc_hold(new_invoice, reason="QC testing in progress")
        continue

    match_result = compare_three_way(po, grn, new_invoice)

    if match_result.all_within_tolerance():
        if new_invoice.total <= auto_approve_threshold(new_invoice.has_po):
            approve(new_invoice)
        else:
            route_for_approval(new_invoice, match_result)
    else:
        for exception in match_result.exceptions:
            route_exception(new_invoice, exception)
```

## 6. Metrics and Monitoring

### Match Rate KPIs

| Metric | Target | Calculation |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| First-pass match rate | >80% | Invoices matching on first attempt / total invoices |
| Exception rate | <20% | Invoices requiring exception handling / total invoices |
| Auto-approval rate | >60% | Invoices auto-approved / total invoices |
| Average resolution time | <3 days | Mean time from exception flag to resolution |
| Straight-through processing | >70% | Invoices from receipt to approved without human touch |

### Root Cause Analysis for Exceptions

| Root Cause | Frequency | Fix |
|-----------|-----------|-----|
| PO not created before ordering | 15-20% | Enforce PO-first policy; block payment without PO |
| Price change not updated on PO | 10-15% | Quarterly PO price review; commodity auto-update |
| Receiving quantity error | 5-10% | Warehouse training; barcode scanning at receiving |
| Vendor billing error | 5-10% | Vendor scorecard; error rate tracking |
| UOM mismatch | 3-5% | Standardize UOM in vendor master and item master |
| Late GRN entry | 5-10% | SLA for warehouse receiving data entry (same day) |
| QC hold extending beyond SLA | 3-5% | QC capacity planning; prioritization rules |

### Vendor Scorecard Impact
Three-way match performance feeds vendor scorecards:
- **Invoice accuracy rate**: % of invoices matching PO on first pass
- **Delivery accuracy**: % of shipments matching PO quantity within tolerance
- **Quality acceptance rate**: % of lots passing QC on first test
- **Documentation compliance**: % of shipments with complete COA, organic cert, allergen declaration
- Vendors below performance thresholds trigger: corrective action request, secondary sourcing, or vendor replacement evaluation
