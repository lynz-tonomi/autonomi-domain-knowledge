# F&B Product Formulation

Deep beverage formulation science for LynZ. Covers the chemistry, physics, and engineering of commercial beverage development.

## When to Use
- Brix, pH, titratable acidity, water activity questions
- Ingredient interaction and compatibility issues
- Shelf life prediction and accelerated testing
- Flavor system design (natural, WONF, organic)
- Preservative hurdle technology
- Sweetener systems (sugar, HFCS, stevia, monk fruit, allulose)
- Emulsion stability, cloud agents, mouthfeel
- Thermal processing (pasteurization, UHT, aseptic, HPP)

## Key Concepts
- **Brix**: Soluble solids measured by refractometer. Target varies: juice (11-16°), carbonated (10-13°), functional (6-10°)
- **pH**: Critical for safety AND flavor. Below 4.6 = acid food (no C. botulinum risk). Most beverages 2.5-4.2
- **Water Activity (aw)**: Controls microbial growth. Beverages typically 0.98-0.99. Syrups/concentrates 0.80-0.85
- **Titratable Acidity**: Total acid content vs pH (which is free H+). Better predictor of perceived sourness
- **Degrees Brix/Acid Ratio**: Primary driver of flavor balance. Orange juice: 12-16 ratio. Apple: 20-30 ratio
