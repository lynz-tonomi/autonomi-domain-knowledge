## 3. Organic Handling SOP Template

### SOP-ORG-001: Organic Product Receiving

**Purpose:** Ensure all incoming organic ingredients are verified, documented, and properly segregated upon receipt.

**Scope:** Applies to all receiving dock personnel handling organic ingredient deliveries.

**Responsibilities:**
- Receiving clerk: Executes procedure
- QA supervisor: Verifies compliance, reviews logs
- QA director: Approves deviations

**Procedure:**
1. Check delivery documentation:
   - Bill of lading / packing slip
   - Organic certificate (verify current, not expired, correct scope covers the ingredient)
   - Lot/batch identification on packaging matches documentation
   - Transaction certificate (for imported organic — SOE requirement)
2. Verify organic labeling on all packages:
   - Certifier logo present
   - "Organic" or "Certified Organic" statement on packaging or labels
   - Lot numbers legible
3. Inspect condition:
   - No damage, contamination, or compromised packaging
   - Temperature check (if applicable)
   - No signs of pest activity
4. Record in receiving log:
   - Date received
   - Supplier name
   - Ingredient name
   - Lot number(s)
   - Quantity received
   - Organic certificate number and expiry
   - Inspector initials
   - Accept/reject decision
5. Move to designated organic storage area immediately
6. If ANY issue found:
   - Segregate shipment in "hold" area
   - Notify QA supervisor
   - Do NOT use ingredient until issue is resolved
   - Document deviation

**Records:**
- Organic Receiving Log (retain 5 years)
- Supplier organic certificates (retain 5 years)
- Deviation log (if applicable)

## 4. Ingredient Receiving & Inspection SOP

### SOP-ORG-002: Ingredient Inspection for Organic Compliance

**Purpose:** Verify that each ingredient lot complies with the Organic System Plan before use in production.

**Procedure:**
1. Before using any ingredient in organic production, verify:
   - Ingredient is listed in the approved OSP ingredient list
   - If organic: supplier organic certificate is current and covers this ingredient
   - If non-agricultural (§205.605): product or substance has been reviewed and approved by certifying agent
   - If §205.606 ingredient: commercial unavailability documentation is current
2. Cross-reference lot number with receiving log
3. Verify ingredient packaging still shows organic identification (label/logo intact)
4. If ingredient is NOT in approved OSP:
   - STOP — do not use
   - Notify QA director
   - Submit OSP amendment to certifying agent if ingredient needs to be added
5. Document check in pre-production verification log

## 8. Label Review Checklist

### For Products Labeled "100% Organic" (100% organic ingredients)
- [ ] All ingredients are certified organic
- [ ] "100% Organic" statement on principal display panel
- [ ] USDA Organic seal may be displayed
- [ ] Certifying agent name and address (or seal) displayed on information panel
- [ ] Ingredient statement lists all ingredients as "organic [ingredient name]"
- [ ] No non-organic ingredients present (excluding water and salt)

### For Products Labeled "Organic" (≥95% organic ingredients)
- [ ] At least 95% of ingredients are organic (by weight, excluding water and salt)
- [ ] "Organic" in product name on principal display panel
- [ ] USDA Organic seal may be displayed
- [ ] Certifying agent name and address (or seal) displayed on information panel
- [ ] Ingredient statement identifies organic ingredients with "organic" preceding ingredient name
- [ ] Non-organic ingredients (≤5%) comply with National List (§205.605 or §205.606)
- [ ] Commercial unavailability documented for any §205.606 ingredients

### For Products Labeled "Made With Organic [specified ingredients]" (≥70% organic)
- [ ] At least 70% of ingredients are organic (by weight, excluding water and salt)
- [ ] "Made With Organic [ingredient name(s)]" on principal display panel (up to 3 ingredient groups)
- [ ] USDA Organic seal NOT allowed
- [ ] Certifying agent name and address (or seal) displayed on information panel
- [ ] Ingredient statement identifies organic ingredients
- [ ] Remaining ingredients NOT required to be on National List (but must not be produced using excluded methods, sewage sludge, or ionizing radiation)

### For Products with <70% Organic Ingredients
- [ ] No "organic" claims on principal display panel
- [ ] NO USDA Organic seal
- [ ] Certifying agent identification NOT required (and should not be displayed)
- [ ] May identify organic ingredients in ingredient statement only (e.g., "organic cane sugar")
- [ ] No organic production claims anywhere on package

### General Label Requirements (All Categories)
- [ ] Ingredient statement in descending order of predominance
- [ ] All required FDA labeling elements present (Nutrition Facts, allergens, etc.)
- [ ] Net weight/volume statement
- [ ] Manufacturer/distributor name and address
- [ ] No misleading claims

## 13. Allergen/Gluten Cross-Contact Prevention SOP

### SOP-GF-001: Gluten-Free Cross-Contact Prevention

**Purpose:** Prevent gluten cross-contact in products designated as gluten-free.

**Scope:** All production, storage, and shipping personnel.

**Definitions:**
- Gluten: Proteins found in wheat, barley, rye, and their crossbred hybrids
- Cross-contact: Unintentional incorporation of gluten into a gluten-free product through shared equipment, surfaces, utensils, or environmental exposure

**Procedure:**

1. **Ingredient Control:**
   - All ingredients for gluten-free products must be verified gluten-free (COA, supplier declaration, or testing)
   - High-risk ingredients (oats, spice blends, flavors, starches) require additional verification
   - Incoming ingredients for gluten-free production stored in designated area, separated from gluten-containing ingredients

2. **Production Scheduling:**
   - Gluten-free products produced FIRST after deep clean (before any gluten-containing products)
   - If dedicated line is not available, gluten-containing products run LAST in production sequence
   - Schedule documented on production calendar

3. **Equipment Cleaning:**
   - Validated cleaning procedure between gluten-containing and gluten-free runs
   - Cleaning procedure: [describe specific steps — disassemble, wash, rinse, sanitize, inspect]
   - Post-clean verification:
     - Visual inspection (documented)
     - Allergen swab test (lateral flow device for gluten, e.g., Romer AgraStrip, Neogen Reveal) — must show <10 ppm (for GFCO) or <20 ppm (for NSF/FDA)
     - Results recorded in cleaning verification log
   - If swab test fails: re-clean and re-test. Do NOT proceed with gluten-free production until test passes.

4. **Environmental Controls:**
   - Air handling: flour dust containment measures in place
   - Separate utensils, scoops, and containers for gluten-free production (color-coded or clearly labeled)
   - Employee hand-washing and glove change required before handling gluten-free products
   - No open containers of gluten-containing ingredients in gluten-free production area during gluten-free runs

5. **Finished Product Testing:**
   - Finished product tested per GFCO/NSF certification requirements
   - Method: R5 Sandwich ELISA (or as specified by certifier)
   - Frequency: per certification requirements (every batch, every lot, or as specified)
   - Results documented and retained

6. **Employee Training:**
   - All production employees trained on gluten cross-contact prevention
   - Training covers: what gluten is, why cross-contact matters, specific procedures, what to do if cross-contact suspected
   - Annual refresher training
   - Training records maintained

**Records:**
- Production schedule (showing gluten-free sequencing)
- Cleaning verification logs (including swab test results)
- Finished product test results
- Training records
- Deviation reports (any suspected cross-contact events)
