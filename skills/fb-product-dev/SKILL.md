# F&B Product Development -- Deep Industry Knowledge Base

## Purpose

This knowledge base encodes real-world food and beverage new product development (NPD) expertise. It is not platform documentation. It covers the full lifecycle from ideation through post-launch review, with particular depth in beverage commercialization -- the domain where stage-gate discipline, sensory science, manufacturing scale-up, and FDA regulatory compliance intersect daily.

## Key Concepts

### 1. Stage-Gate NPD Process
The industry-standard framework for moving a product from idea to shelf. Five stages separated by management gates where projects are funded, redirected, or killed. Typical F&B timeline: 12-18 months concept-to-shelf for a line extension; 18-24 months for a new brand or novel ingredient platform.

**Reference:** `references/npd_stage_gate.md`

### 2. Sensory Evaluation
The science of measuring human perception of food and beverage attributes. Encompasses trained descriptive analysis (objective measurement), discrimination testing (difference detection), and consumer affective testing (liking and preference). Proper sensory work is the bridge between R&D bench formulation and consumer acceptance.

**Reference:** `references/sensory_evaluation.md`

### 3. Scale-Up Manufacturing
The engineering and operations challenge of moving from a bench-top beaker to a commercial production line. Covers pilot trials, co-manufacturer qualification, process authority requirements, and quality monitoring during production. The #1 cause of NPD delays in beverage is failed or repeated production trials.

**Reference:** `references/scale_up_manufacturing.md`

### 4. Labeling & Claims
FDA regulatory framework governing what appears on the package. Includes Nutrition Facts formatting, ingredient statements, nutrient content claims, health claims, structure/function claims, organic certification tiers, and state-specific requirements (Prop 65, NY sodium). Getting claims wrong can require a full label reprint -- a $50K-$200K mistake depending on SKU count and inventory.

**Reference:** `references/labeling_claims.md`

## Cross-Cutting Principles

1. **Gate discipline saves money.** Killing a bad project at Gate 1 costs $5K-$15K in sunk R&D time. Killing it after a failed production trial costs $75K-$250K. Killing it after launch costs $500K+.

2. **Sensory is not optional.** Internal "taste tests" with employees are not sensory science. Every product touching a consumer shelf needs at minimum a CLT with n >= 100 target consumers using validated scales.

3. **Label compliance is a pre-launch blocker.** The label goes to legal and regulatory review at Stage 3, not Stage 5. Regulatory surprises at launch are among the most common and most avoidable NPD failures.

4. **Co-man relationships are partnerships, not transactions.** The best co-manufacturers are booked 6-12 months out. Early engagement (Stage 2) and clear trial protocols prevent costly re-runs.

5. **Shelf life determines launch timing.** A 12-month shelf life study cannot be compressed. Accelerated studies (ASLT) can provide directional data, but retailers require real-time confirmation before authorizing distribution.

## Usage Notes

- These references contain **industry knowledge**, not company-specific SOPs. Adapt gate criteria, timelines, and financial thresholds to the specific organization.
- CFR citations are current as of early 2025. FDA rulemaking (e.g., updated Nutrition Facts, front-of-pack labeling proposals) may alter specific requirements.
- Co-manufacturer minimums, pricing, and lead times are directional benchmarks. Always confirm with specific co-man partners.
