## Overview & Framework

The stage-gate process for F&B product development divides commercialization into five distinct phases separated by decision gates. Each gate enforces cross-functional accountability and ensures only viable projects consume resources. The process balances speed-to-market with risk mitigation, recognizing that formulation changes, regulatory delays, and supply chain constraints can extend timelines unpredictably.

**Key Principles:**
- Projects must meet minimum exit criteria at each gate or be killed/recycled
- Gate decisions are made by cross-functional committee, not individual functions
- Recycled projects return to specific stage with defined deliverables, not automatic re-reviews
- Time compression favors risk-managed front-loading over crisis management downstream
- Post-launch metrics inform portfolio health and future pipeline management

## Gate 0: Ideation & Concept Screening

Gate 0 is the highest-volume gate, intentionally processing 80-120 concepts annually to fund 6-12 projects into Gate 1. This gate emphasizes rapid screening, disciplined kill criteria, and white space identification.

### Consumer Insights & Trend Analysis Sources

**Primary Market Intelligence Platforms:**

- **IRI/Circana Panel Data**: Multi-retail scanner data (Walmart, Target, grocery chains, drugstores representing ~80% of U.S. CPG volume). Essential for category trending, price elasticity, and competitive share momentum. Typical lead time for custom reports: 2-3 weeks. Subscription cost: $80K-200K annually depending on category depth.

- **Mintel Innovation Database**: Tracks new product launches, claims, packaging formats, and segment premiumization. Covers ~140K food launches globally annually with detailed filtration by claim (functional, natural, clean label, etc.). Alerts on emerging derivatives of trending products.

- **SPINS (Natural & Better-for-You Retailer Data)**: Covers natural/organic retailers (Whole Foods, natural chains) and mainstream retailers' natural sections. Segment-specific growth rates often 3-8 years ahead of conventional channels—critical for early trend validation. Real-time velocity updates.

- **Social Listening (Brandwatch, Sprout Social, Talkwalker)**: Captures consumer conversation volume, sentiment, and emerging terminology. Monitor category-related conversations at 1,000+ mentions monthly threshold to establish baseline. Red flags: if product-adjacent conversations show consistently negative sentiment (e.g., "protein bars" + "tastes chalky" at >15% of conversation), concept refinement needed pre-Gate 1.

- **NPD/CAGR Analysis**: Leverage Mintel, Nielsen, Circana segment-specific reports. "Better-for-you" snacking CAGR 4.2% (2020-2025); plant-based proteins CAGR 8.1%; functional beverages CAGR 5.7%. Compare concept's addressable segment growth rate against company hurdle rates (typically 3-5% for adjacent innovation, 10%+ for transformational).

**Trend Lifecycle Framework:**

Classify trends across three horizons:

1. **Macro Trends** (3-7 year cycles): Sustainability, health/wellness, premiumization, convenience, personalization. Align concept to minimum 2-3 macro trends for resilience.
2. **Micro Trends** (1-3 year cycles): Specific claim clusters (e.g., "adaptogenic" functionality, "regenerative agriculture" sourcing). High velocity but short runway.
3. **Hype vs. Secular**: Distinguish temporary surge from sustained adoption. Rule of thumb: if trend volume peaked 18+ months ago and plateau has begun, classify as secular; if still ascending, assume hype risk.

**White Space Mapping Methodology:**

Construct matrix: X-axis = primary consumer benefit (functional, indulgent, convenient, sustainable); Y-axis = price tier (budget, mainstream, premium, luxury). Overlay existing product portfolio and competitive landscape (top 10-15 SKUs by segment). Identify quadrants with <2 active competitors, <$10M segment size (below noise level), and growth rate >5%. This defines white space zones justified for exploration. Quadrants with >5 competitors and <2% segment growth are "red zones"—avoid unless transformational differentiation exists.

### Concept Screening Criteria & Scoring

**Weighted Scoring Matrix** (Total: 100 points; minimum threshold to advance: 65 points)

| Criterion | Weight | Scale | Rationale |
|-----------|--------|-------|-----------|
| **Strategic Fit** | 25% | 1-10 | Alignment with core capabilities, brand architecture, distribution assets, margin profile |
| **Technical Feasibility** | 20% | 1-10 | Ingredient availability, formulation precedent, manufacturing complexity, no fundamental showstoppers |
| **Market Size & Growth** | 20% | 1-10 | Addressable market >$50M within 3 years; segment CAGR >3% |
| **Competitive Intensity** | 15% | 1-10 | Inverse scored: >5 entrenched competitors = 3/10; 1-2 competitors = 8/10 |
| **Regulatory Pathway** | 10% | 1-10 | Confidence in FDA/state approval; novel ingredients = lower score; all-GRAS ingredients = 9-10 |
| **Margin Potential** | 10% | 1-10 | Estimated COGS cost of goods sold) 35-45% of retail price = 8-10; >55% = 3-5 |

**Example Scoring:**
A ready-to-drink adaptogenic coffee concept scores: Strategic Fit 8 (aligns with energy/wellness portfolio, existing RTD infrastructure), Technical Feasibility 7 (functional ingredients proven in market, requires taste optimization), Market Size 8 ($2.1B RTD coffee market, functional segment growing 6% CAGR), Competitive Intensity 5 (3-4 established players: Rise Brewing, Baristas, Remedy), Regulatory 9 (functional ingredients: L-theanine, ashwagandha—both GRAS; no NDI issues), Margin 6 (premium positioning supports 40% COGS, but ingredient costs trending up). Weighted score: (8×0.25) + (7×0.20) + (8×0.20) + (5×0.15) + (9×0.10) + (6×0.10) = 7.35/10 = **73.5 points → Advance to Gate 1**.

### Gate 0 Outputs: Approved Product Brief

Advance concepts must produce one-page **Approved Product Brief** containing:

1. **Concept Statement**: Target consumer, key benefit, use occasion, differentiation (max 2 sentences)
2. **Preliminary Market Sizing**: TAM/SAM/SOM; CAGR; key competitive set
3. **Strategic Rationale**: Why this concept now; brand fit; synergies with existing portfolio
4. **Preliminary Regulatory Red Flags**: Any ingredients requiring expedited review or claims substantiation
5. **Estimated Development Timeline**: Months to Gate 1, 2, 3, 4 (baseline assumptions)
6. **Gate Score Breakdown**: Transparency on scoring; specific weaknesses to address in Gate 1

### Gate 0 Kill Criteria

Terminate concepts that meet **any** of these criteria:

- **Market Size <$50M**: Segment too small to justify portfolio allocation; exception only if transformational or strategic defensive play
- **Strategic Misfit**: Concept conflicts with brand positioning, margins, or manufacturing/distribution capabilities
- **IP Conflicts**: Pre-existing patents, trademarks, or freedom-to-operate issues cannot be resolved within 6-month pre-Gate 1 window
- **Regulatory Showstopper**: Key ingredient requires food additive petition (FDA timeline: 24-36 months) when market window is <24 months; state-specific bans (e.g., California Prop 65 concern for which no dossier exists)
- **Scoring Threshold**: Weighted score <65/100 indicates misalignment with gating criteria

### Gate 0 Recycle Criteria

Return concepts for refinement if:

- Scoring 55-65 points but exhibiting strong consumer demand signals: Narrow target consumer definition, refocus differentiation, address lowest-scoring criteria (typically regulatory or margin concerns)
- Market sizing ambiguous: Request deeper white space analysis; iterate competitive mapping
- Strategic fit unclear: Allow product team 2-3 weeks to clarify brand positioning alignment and resubmit

## Gate 1: Feasibility

Gate 1 determines whether a concept can be manufactured, meets regulatory requirements, maintains target margins, and has viable supply chain. Projects spend 8-12 weeks in this stage. Output is a **Feasibility Assessment** document signaling readiness for development stage investment (budgeted resource allocation, external lab work, supplier engagement).

### Bench-Top Formulation Requirements

Initiate internal R&D formulation with minimum **three iterations** before Gate 1 review:

1. **Iteration 1 - Ingredient Proof**: Confirm all functional/structural ingredients blend without separation, precipitation, or phase issues. No sensory evaluation; lab analysis only (appearance, pH, viscosity, or relevant physical properties). Timeline: 1 week.

2. **Iteration 2 - Prototype Refinement**: Adjust ratios based on Iteration 1 learnings. Conduct preliminary in-house sensory screening (internal panel, n=5-8 trained panelists). Evaluate on 9-point hedonic scale for flavor, texture, mouthfeel, aftertaste. Pass threshold: ≥6.0 on overall acceptance; no >1 point variance across panelists for key attributes (indicate formulation instability).

3. **Iteration 3 - Gate 1 Submission**: Final bench formulation. Conduct external third-party sensory screening (n=15-25 panelists, typical cost $2K-4K) using **Central Location Test (CLT)** protocol if rapid feedback needed (1-2 day turnaround) or Home Use Test (HUT, n=25-35, 5-7 day evaluation period, $4K-6K) for realistic consumption context. Acceptance threshold remains ≥6.0 hedonic; if comparing to benchmark competitor product, target ≥6.5 vs. benchmark's ≥7.0 acceptable (indicates improvement pathway exists).

**Regulatory Requirement**: All formulation iterations must reference ingredient GRAS determinations and safety data sheets (SDS). Flag any ingredients lacking published GRAS status or requiring pre-market approval.

### Preliminary COGS Model

Construct bottom-up COGS model using actual quoted ingredient costs (not index-based estimates). Model structure:

**Formula COGS per unit (typical 12 oz beverage example):**

| Component | Cost | % of Total COGS |
|-----------|------|-----------------|
| **Raw Materials** | | |
| Base ingredient (liquid) | $0.18 | 22% |
| Functional ingredients (3-5 components) | $0.22 | 27% |
| Flavors, color, preservation | $0.08 | 10% |
| Subtotal Raw Materials | $0.48 | 59% |
| **Packaging** | | |
| Bottle/can + closure | $0.12 | 15% |
| Label (pre-printed) | $0.04 | 5% |
| Carton/shipper | $0.03 | 4% |
| Subtotal Packaging | $0.19 | 24% |
| **Manufacturing & Overhead** | | |
| Co-pack conversion cost | $0.08 | 10% |
| Freight (ingredient inbound + outbound) | $0.04 | 5% |
| Subtotal Mfg & Overhead | $0.12 | 15% |
| **TOTAL COGS per unit** | **$0.79** | **100%** |

**Margin Analysis:**
- Suggested Retail Price (SRP): $2.49 (premium functional beverage positioning)
- Wholesale to retail: ~55-60% of SRP = $1.37-1.49
- Company net sales (after wholesale margin): $1.37 SRP reference; COGS $0.79 = **42% COGS to sales ratio**
- Gross margin (before overhead): $0.58 (58%)
- **Target margin threshold**: 50-60% gross margin; projects <50% are margin-constrained and recycle to reformulation

**Cost Reduction Levers & Gate 1 Contingency:**
- Ingredient commodity volatility: Include +/- 5% sensitivity range on functional ingredients (oils, extracts, botanicals subject to crop variation)
- Co-pack efficiency: Quote from 2-3 contract manufacturers; confirm capacity and minimum batch sizes (typically 5,000-10,000 units per production run)
- Volume ramp assumptions: Note which COGS elements scale (packaging printing, conversion labor) vs. fixed (supplier NRE, setup fees); recalculate projected COGS at Y2 volume targets

**Kill Criterion**: COGS >120% of target retail margins. Example: if target margin is 50% and SRP is $2.49, allowable COGS is $1.25; calculated COGS of $1.50 (120% of allowable) triggers kill/recycle.

### Regulatory Screen Checklist

Execute comprehensive pre-Gate 1 regulatory review. Document findings in **Regulatory Status Summary** (1-2 pages).

**Ingredient-Level Review:**

- [ ] **FDA Status for All Ingredients**: Verify each ingredient is either:
  - GRAS affirmed (color additives, food additives with published GRAS opinions)
  - Permitted food additive (in FDA's Code of Federal Regulations Title 21 CFR Part 182-186)
  - Dietary supplement ingredient with NDIN (NDI) notification history (if relevant; must review FDA NDIN Submissions database for completeness)

- [ ] **GRAS Determination Pathways**:
  - Ingredient with published consensus GRAS status: No further action
  - Ingredient with manufacturer GRAS conclusion (self-affirmed): Assess sufficiency of supporting dossier; flag if limited publisly available safety data exists
  - Novel ingredient or novel use: Requires potential FDA GRAS petition or NDI notification (32-48 week timeline); assess market window tolerance

- [ ] **Allergen Profile**: Identify all major allergens (milk, eggs, fish, shellfish, tree nuts, peanuts, wheat, soy, sesame). Assess manufacturing cross-contact risk if co-packing facility handles allergen-containing products; request supplier certification of allergen control program (FSMA Preventive Controls qualified individual approval).

- [ ] **State-Specific Regulatory Issues**:
  - California Prop 65: Flag if formula contains substances on Prop 65 list (acrylamide, cadmium, lead, certain plant extracts); assess labeling impact or ingredient substitution necessity
  - Texas Health & Safety Code §431.002: If fortified, ensure vitamin/mineral additions comply with state micronutrient limits
  - Canada/Mexico entry plans: Note divergent regulatory frameworks (Canada MNHP licensing for functional claims; Mexico COFEPRIS approval timelines)

- [ ] **Claims Feasibility**:
  - Identify preliminary label claims (functional, structure-function, nutrient content, health claims if applicable)
  - Map to applicable regulatory framework (FDA structure-function claims require qualified expert substantiation; conventional health claims require significant scientific agreement)
  - Flag any claims requiring bioavailability or clinical efficacy data; assess study timeline and cost ($100K-500K for published RCT)

**Regulatory Output**: Approved concepts receive **Regulatory Status Summary** classifying pathway risk as Green (all GRAS, clear claims path), Yellow (one ingredient with published GRAS but limited U.S. safety precedent, claims require substantiation), or Red (novel ingredient requiring petition, uncertain claims pathway, state-specific barriers). Green and Yellow projects advance; Red projects recycle or escalate for executive decision on filing costs and timeline extension tolerance.

### IP & Freedom-to-Operate Screen

Conduct formulation-level IP due diligence:

- **Patent Search**: Query USPTO, international patent databases for concept-adjacent patents. Search terms: product category + functional ingredient + delivery form. Flag if broad composition patents exist with >5 years remaining exclusivity.
- **Trademark Clearance**: Confirm proposed product name availability via USPTO TESS and common law searches; cost $500-1K.
- **Manufacturing Method Patents**: Assess if proprietary process claims exist in space; flag if company manufacturing method infringes non-expired patent.

**Kill Criterion**: If freedom-to-operate issues cannot be resolved via design-around or licensing within 6 months and reasonable cost (<10% of projected Year 1 revenue), project is killed.

### Preliminary Supply Chain Assessment

For each critical ingredient (typically top 3-5 by cost or functionality):

- **Availability & Lead Times**: Supplier quote required; assess lead time from order to delivery (standard: 4-8 weeks for custom ingredient lots; botanicals/extracts: 8-16 weeks due to sourcing/processing)
- **Sourcing Complexity**: Rate as Low (commodity ingredient, multiple suppliers, >3 qualified sources), Medium (specialty ingredient, 2-3 suppliers, some processing required), or High (boutique/novel ingredient, single supplier, long lead time)
- **Volume Scalability**: Confirm supplier can support Year 1 volume projection (assume 500K-2M units) and Year 3 expansion (2-5M units) without significant price increase or forced reformulation
- **Regulatory Compliance**: Verify supplier holds necessary certifications (FDA facility registration, ISO 22000 food safety certification, organic/non-GMO certifications if relevant)

**Recycle Criterion**: If single-source critical ingredient has lead time >6 months or no committed supplier capacity for projected volumes, recycle to Gate 0 for alternative ingredient identification or timeline re-planning.

### Gate 1 Outputs: Feasibility Assessment Document

Advance to Gate 2 only with complete **Feasibility Assessment** containing:

1. **Validated Formulation**: Final bench formula (ingredient list, quantities, order-of-addition documentation)
2. **Regulatory Status Summary**: GRAS determinations, allergen profile, state-specific risks, claims substantiation plan
3. **COGS Model**: Bottom-up build with cost breakdown by category; margin sensitivity analysis
4. **Supply Chain Readiness**: Supplier quotes, lead times, scalability assessment for critical ingredients
5. **Sensory Data**: CLT/HUT results (n, methodology, hedonic scores, key findings); comparison to benchmark if applicable
6. **Development Plan & Timeline**: Preliminary phasing to Gates 2-4; key milestone dates; resource requirements

### Gate 1 Kill Criteria

Terminate projects exhibiting:

- **COGS >120% of Target**: Reformulation cannot achieve margin requirements without fundamental concept change
- **Regulatory Showstopper**: Key ingredient requires food additive petition with timeline incompatible with market window; state-specific prohibition cannot be resolved
- **Supply Chain Failure**: Critical ingredient has no qualified supplier willing to commit capacity; lead times exceed 6 months
- **IP/Freedom-to-Operate Conflict**: Unresolvable patent or trademark issues block commercialization

### Gate 1 Recycle Criteria

Return to formulation/feasibility work if:

- **COGS 100-120% of Target**: Reformulate (substitute lower-cost ingredient maintaining functional profile, reduce inclusions levels, adjust packaging format). Typical reformulation cycle: 2-4 weeks.
- **Regulatory Path Exists But Complex**: Single ingredient requires GAS petition or claims require clinical data; project timeline extends 6-12 months but commercial opportunity remains viable. Adjust project schedule and resource plan; return to Gate 1 review with updated feasibility assessment.
- **Sensory Marginal (5.5-6.0 Hedonic)**: Flavor/texture adjustment required; conduct Iteration 4 bench work (1-2 weeks) with reformulation, retest, return to Gate 1.

## Gate 2: Development

Gate 2 transitions concept to commercially producible product. Typical duration: 16-24 weeks. Cross-functional team expands to include Operations (process development), Quality, Regulatory, and external partners (sensory firms, contract labs). Gate 2 exits into commercialization readiness review (Gate 3).

### Pilot Plant Trial Requirements

Conduct **minimum three pilot-scale production runs** at contract manufacturing partner's facility or in-house pilot plant:

**Pilot Run 1: Process Parameters & Parameter Optimization**
- Scale: Typically 25-50% of target batch size (e.g., if commercial batch = 10,000 units, pilot batch = 2,500-5,000 units)
- Focus: Confirm ingredient incorporation sequence, mixing speeds/times, temperature control windows, hold times, and fill/packaging accuracy
- Documentation: Detailed batch log with timestamps, equipment readings (temperature, pressure, agitation speeds), ingredient additions, and in-process observations
- Analysis: Measure finished product quality attributes (pH, viscosity, color, particle size/suspension if applicable); confirm no phase separation, sedimentation, or unexpected reactions
- Output: Approved process parameters (equipment settings, times, temperatures) and revised process flow diagram

**Pilot Run 2: Process Validation & Scale Consistency**
- Repeat Run 1 parameters; compare output to confirm reproducibility
- Conduct process capability study: measure critical quality attributes at 5-10 points within batch and across multiple subsamples. Calculate process capability index (Cpk):
  - Cpk = (USL - Mean) / (3 × StdDev) where USL = upper specification limit
  - Target Cpk ≥1.33 (indicates process can produce 99.73% conforming units with <0.135% defect rate)
  - If Cpk <1.0, process is incapable; require parameter adjustment and Pilot Run 3 repeat
- Document any deviation and corrective action

**Pilot Run 3: Full Validation & Sufficient Data for Production Authorization**
- Execute final pilot run confirming Cpk ≥1.33 across quality attributes
- Generate sufficient data to support production run authorization (FDA expects minimum 3 consecutive process runs demonstrating control for thermal/acidified foods; internal procedures may require higher n for high-risk products)
- Retain pilot batch samples for shelf-life studies and quality reference standards

### Shelf-Life Study Initiation

Initiate formal shelf-life program during Gate 2 to generate data in parallel with formulation optimization (results inform Gate 3 label dating):

**Protocol Design Elements:**

- **Study Type**: Dual protocol (accelerated + real-time)
  - Accelerated: Store at elevated temperature (e.g., 40°C/104°F for beverage, 45°C/113°F for shelf-stable foods) with sampling at 2, 4, 8, 12 week intervals. Shorter timeline (3-4 months) provides preliminary data.
  - Real-time: Store at target retail conditions (room temperature 70-72°F for shelf-stable; refrigerated 41°F for chilled). Extended timeline (9-12 months minimum) captures actual aging.

- **Sample Pull Schedule**:
  - Accelerated: Weeks 0, 2, 4, 8, 12 (minimum 5 time points)
  - Real-time: Months 0, 1, 3, 6, 9, 12+ (minimum 6 time points; extend beyond anticipated expiration date to confirm degradation trend)

- **Analytical Parameters** (customized to product type; examples for ready-to-drink beverage):
  - Microbiology: Aerobic plate count, yeast/mold, coliforms (if not sterile); frequency typically Month 0, 3, 6, 9, 12 for real-time
  - Chemistry: pH (primary degradation driver), color (CIELAB values, Delta E <2.0 acceptable), viscosity if applicable, volatile components via GC if flavor stability critical
  - Sensory: Organoleptic (flavor, aroma, appearance, mouthfeel) by trained panel (n=5-8) at each sampling interval; acceptance threshold maintained at ≥6.0 hedonic
  - Nutrient Content: If fortified/functional, verify potency stability for key vitamins/minerals at baseline and 3-month, 6-month, 12-month intervals (target retention ≥90% for water-soluble vitamins; ≥85% for fat-soluble)

- **Statistical Extrapolation**:
  - Fit Arrhenius model to accelerated data if studying chemical degradation: Log(degradation rate) vs. 1/Temperature. Extrapolate to retail storage temperature to estimate real-time progression.
  - Example: If accelerated study (40°C) shows pH decline of 0.5 units over 12 weeks, Arrhenius model may predict 12-month real-time decline of 0.1-0.15 units depending on temperature sensitivity coefficient.

**Shelf-Life Output**: Final shelf-life study summary with:
- Selected expiration date (defined as date when ≥90% of samples meet quality specifications; or 18-24 months for shelf-stable, 12-16 months for refrigerated as typical thresholds)
- Stability-limiting attribute (often pH, microbial, or sensory)
- Storage condition statement for label ("Store in cool, dry place"; "Refrigerate after opening")
- Recommendations for packaging material if multiple options evaluated (barrier properties, headspace management for oxidation-sensitive formulas)

### Packaging Development Milestones

Execute in parallel with formulation work:

**Milestone 1 - Format Selection** (Week 1-2):
- Evaluate 2-3 package formats against functional and market positioning requirements
- Example decision matrix for ready-to-drink functional beverage: Aseptic carton vs. PET bottle vs. glass bottle
  - Aseptic carton: Lower COGS ($0.08-0.12/unit), no refrigeration required, longer shelf-life, but limited brand visibility, difficult home storage/reclosure
  - PET bottle: Higher COGS ($0.14-0.18/unit), requires refrigeration, shorter shelf-life (~12-16 months), but superior brand presentation and established consumer category expectations
  - Glass: Premium positioning, higher COGS ($0.18-0.25/unit), fragility/shipping risk, but environmental benefits
- Select primary format + secondary format option if pursuing multiple retail channels (e.g., aseptic for foodservice, PET for retail)

**Milestone 2 - Compatibility Testing** (Week 2-8):
- Conduct package material compatibility studies to confirm formula does not degrade, discolor, or absorb into packaging over expected shelf-life
- For PET/polycarbonate: Test against organic compounds (essential oils, flavors, colors) which may migrate or absorb into polymer; generate data per ASTM F1908 (permeation testing) if relevant
- For aseptic carton: Verify aluminum/polyethylene laminate does not react with acidic or high-sodium formulas
- Output: Confirmed package material compatibility; any reformulation required to accommodate packaging limitations identified

**Milestone 3 - Artwork & Label Design** (Week 4-12):
- Initiate artwork brief to design agency specifying:
  - Regulatory text elements (ingredient list, allergen declarations, nutrition facts, manufacturer address, net quantity statement)
  - Marketing claims (functional, clean label, certifications)
  - Bar code/GS1 requirements
- Conduct label compliance review in parallel (Regulatory function validates claims substantiation, allergen declarations, and macro/micronutrient statement accuracy pre-production)
- Cost: Professional label design typically $1.5K-3K; final artwork review and pre-print proofs $500-1K

### Sensory Evaluation Framework

Execute consumer sensory testing to validate product acceptance and competitive positioning:

**Consumer Panel Design:**

- **Target Demographic**: Define usage frequency and category affinity. Example: "Functional beverage category consumers who consume ≥1 functional/energy beverage per week, age 18-45, household income >$50K"
- **Recruiting & Panel Size**:
  - CLT (Central Location Test): n=50-75; respondents evaluate product and benchmark(s) in controlled facility environment (focus group facility, research firm office). Timeline: 1-2 days; cost $3K-5K. Best for rapid feedback pre-Gate 2, formulation adjustments.
  - HUT (Home Use Test): n=25-35; respondents receive product to consume at home over 5-7 days (1-2 consumption occasions minimum). Timeline: 7-14 days; cost $4K-6K. Captures realistic consumption context, repeat consumption intent.

- **Evaluation Methodology**:
  - 9-Point Hedonic Scale: 1 (dislike extremely) to 9 (like extremely); midpoint 5 = "neither like nor dislike"
  - Key Attribute Ratings: Flavor intensity (too weak/just right/too strong), sweetness, texture/mouthfeel, aftertaste, overall quality vs. benchmark
  - Purchase Intent: "Would you buy this product?" (5-point scale: definitely would not to definitely would)
  - Repeat Consumption: For HUT, track actual consumption frequency; incomplete consumption indicates unacceptability

**Acceptance Thresholds:**
- Minimum acceptability: ≥6.0 on 9-point hedonic scale (reflects "like slightly" to "like moderately")
- Competitive parity vs. benchmark: If benchmark product scores 7.2, target formula must score ≥6.5 (acceptable gap depends on differentiation strategy; parity or superiority expected for premium positioning)
- Benchmark win criteria: ≥7.0 on hedonic + >50% "definitely would buy" purchase intent indicates strong competitive advantage

**Diagnostic Data:**
- Identify attribute-level weaknesses (e.g., if aftertaste scores 4.8 vs. overall hedonic 6.2, flavor chemistry adjustment required)
- Document verbatim comments for flavor direction; categorize into themes (e.g., "too sweet" n=8, "artificial aftertaste" n=6, "perfect sweetness" n=12)
- Recycle criterion: If hedonic <6.0 or specific attribute significantly underperforms, return to Gate 1 formulation with targeted refinement (flavor adjustments typically 1-2 week cycle)

### Process Documentation & Control

Compile **Process Validation & Control Plan** for Operations/Quality sign-off:

- **Process Flow Diagram**: Visual representation of ingredient additions, mixing, heating/cooling, hold times, fill line parameters, sealing, coding, packaging/shipping. Include decision points and control checks.
- **Critical Control Points (CCP)**: Identify steps where deviation poses product safety/quality risk
  - Example for beverage: Thermal treatment (if applicable) is CCP for microbiological control; fill line temperature is CCP for product stability
- **Equipment Specifications**: List all equipment with operating parameters (mixer capacity/speed, heat exchanger setpoint, fill line accuracy, coding equipment)
- **Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)**: Document for each production stage (ingredient receiving/staging, mixing sequence, quality checks, fill/seal parameters, finished product handling)
- **Testing Plan**: Confirm which tests executed in-process (e.g., viscosity measured every batch) vs. finished product (microbiology every batch or monthly lot samples)

### Gate 2 Outputs: Validated Formula & Shelf-Life Study

Projects advancing to Gate 3 must demonstrate:

1. **Validated Formula & Process**: Final formulation with supporting pilot trial data (n=3 runs, Cpk ≥1.33); documented process parameters and control points
2. **Shelf-Life Study in Progress**: Accelerated study complete or nearing completion; real-time study underway with minimum 3-month data
3. **Sensory Validation**: CLT/HUT data (n≥50) confirming ≥6.0 hedonic acceptance or ≥6.5 if vs. benchmark competitor
4. **Package Design Finalized**: Format selected, compatibility confirmed, artwork approved
5. **Quality & Regulatory Documentation**: Preliminary finished product and raw material specifications; regulatory pathway confirmed (no new barriers identified since Gate 1)
6. **Development Plan Updated**: Revised timeline and resource requirements for Gates 3-4

### Gate 2 Kill Criteria

Terminate projects if:

- **Sensory Fails Acceptance**: CLT/HUT hedonic <6.0, or loses significantly to benchmark (≥1.5 points lower on 9-point scale); no clear reformulation path within project timeline
- **Pilot Batch Instability**: Three pilot runs show Cpk <1.0; process parameters cannot achieve required control; manufacturing risk unacceptable
- **Shelf-Life Accelerated Data Predicts <50% of Target**: If accelerated study (40°C, 12 weeks) projects 6-month real-time degradation beyond acceptable limits and Arrhenius extrapolation confirms insufficient shelf-life for distribution/retail window

### Gate 2 Recycle Criteria

Return to development if:

- **Minor Formula Adjustments Needed**: Flavor tuning, sweetness calibration, or texture optimization identified from sensory; requires 1-2 formulation iterations; return with updated sensory data
- **Packaging Format Change Required**: Initial format selection unsuitable (compatibility issues, cost misalignment, market feedback); new format evaluation and retesting of compatibility/shelf-life
- **Shelf-Life Study Inconclusive**: Insufficient real-time data or contradictory accelerated/real-time findings; extend study timeline, return to Gate 2 upon completion

## Gate 3: Validation

Gate 3 confirms commercial manufacturability, final regulatory clearance, supply chain readiness, and profit model accuracy. Duration: 12-20 weeks. Exit Gate 3 with production-ready product and all regulatory approvals in place.

### Production Trial Requirements

Conduct **minimum one full-scale production run** at contract manufacturing partner or internal plant:

**Full-Scale Production Run Parameters:**

- **Scale**: Commercial batch size (e.g., 10,000-50,000 units depending on equipment); typically represents 1-4 week production for SKU
- **Objective**: Demonstrate replicability of pilot process parameters at commercial scale; generate sufficient process data (minimum n=3 consecutive commercial batches preferred) to demonstrate sustained capability (Cpk ≥1.33 across all runs)
- **Documentation**: Batch record with real-time equipment readings, ingredient additions, timing, environmental conditions (facility temperature/humidity), quality check results at in-process and finished goods stages
- **Quality Assurance Sampling**:
  - In-process: Verify pH, viscosity, color, particle size (if applicable) at mid-point and end of batch
  - Finished product: Full analytical profile (microbiology, chemistry, physical properties) from samples drawn post-packaging; sensory evaluation by trained panel
  - Stability sample retention: Reserve minimum 5-year archive of finished product samples (per FDA expectation for shelf-stable foods) or 2-year for refrigerated products in case post-market issues arise

**Batch Yield & Efficiency Analysis:**

- Calculate actual yield: (Finished units produced / Target units per batch) × 100%
- Minimum acceptable yield: ≥90% (indicates acceptable process loss and scrap)
- Recycle criterion: Yield 80-90% (process optimization required); Kill criterion: Yield <80% (fundamental process flaw)
- Cost impact: Each 1% yield loss increases effective COGS by ~0.8-1.2% depending on product economics

### Process Authority Review & Approval

For thermally-processed or acidified food products (pH <4.6), require third-party **Process Authority** review and approval before commercial production. Process authority firms (e.g., Campden BRI, Process Advisory Group) conduct independent validation of thermal processes and acidification procedures to ensure microbiological safety.

**Process Authority Review Timeline:**
- Submission package preparation: 2-3 weeks (compile pilot data, process flow, thermal/acidification calculations)
- Authority review: 4-8 weeks (may request additional data or process modifications)
- Approval letter: Conditional approval pending full-scale trial run data confirmation (usually becomes unconditional after first commercial batch documented)

**Regulatory Basis**: FDA Food Safety Modernization Act (FSMA) Preventive Controls rule (21 CFR Part 117, Subpart C) mandates that thermal processes be designed and implemented by a qualified individual or validated by a process authority.

### First Article Inspection (FAI)

For products with new packaging suppliers or significant supply chain changes, conduct **First Article Inspection** per ANSI/AIAG standards:

**FAI Scope** (typical for food products):
- Packaging material samples (label, carton, bottle, closure) from production run, verified against design specifications
- Dimensional verification: Bar code placement, nutritional facts panel position, ingredient list legibility
- Material composition confirmation: PET bottle resin type, label stock weight/finish
- Pre-production sample approval: Final artwork match; color accuracy; print quality (no smudges, misalignment)

**FAI Cost & Timeline**: $500-1.5K; 1-2 week turnaround. Typical completion time: Week 4-6 of Gate 3 phase.

**FAI Output**: FAI report confirming conformance or identifying required corrections (e.g., label artwork alignment, bar code size adjustment). No production release until FAI signed off.

### Quality Specifications Finalized

Develop comprehensive **Finished Product Specifications** and **Raw Material Specifications** documents:

**Finished Product Spec Example (RTD Beverage):**

| Parameter | Specification | Method |
|-----------|---------------|--------|
| Appearance | Clear, free of visible particles, haze, separation | Visual inspection; particle count via laser counter |
| Color | CIELAB L* 45-55, a* -2 to +2, b* 8-15 | Spectrophotometer; reference standards maintained |
| pH | 3.2-3.5 | pH meter per ASTM F179 |
| Viscosity (25°C) | 1.0-1.2 cP | Brookfield viscometer |
| Total Solids (Brix) | 10.0-11.5 | Refractometer |
| Microbiology (Aerobic Plate Count) | <100 CFU/mL | Standard pour plate method, 30°C, 48 hours |
| Yeast/Mold | <10 CFU/mL | Potato dextrose agar, 25°C, 5 days |
| Coliforms | <10 CFU/mL (or Absent) | Per FDA BAM Chapter 4 |
| Vitamin C (if fortified) | 45-55 mg/8 fl oz (90-110% of label claim) | HPLC per USP monograph |
| Potency (functional ingredient, e.g., ashwagandha) | 250-275 mg/serving (90-110% of label claim) | HPLC with validated method |

**Raw Material Specs**: Define acceptance criteria for each ingredient (supplier lot testing); examples:
- Botanical extract: Identity via TLC, potency assay (HPLC), microbial <1000 CFU/g, E. coli absent
- Vitamin concentrate: Potency within ±5% of certificate of analysis, microbial <1000 CFU/g
- Packaging (PET resin): Intrinsic viscosity 0.70-0.75 dL/g, color <APHA 50, moisture <0.04%

### Regulatory Package Completion

Compile final **Regulatory Compliance Package** for submission to FDA (if applicable) and state agencies:

**Nutrition Facts Label:**
- Verified by third-party lab analysis (samples from production batch)
- Mandatory elements: Serving size, calories, macronutrients (total fat, saturated fat, trans fat, cholesterol, sodium, carbohydrate, fiber, sugars, added sugars, protein), mandatory minerals (calcium, iron, potassium), vitamins per serving
- Example cost: Lab analysis for full nutrient profile (macronutrients + 20 micronutrients) $1.5K-2.5K per sample

**Label Compliance Review:**
- All claims substantiated: "Good source of vitamin C" requires ≥10% Daily Value; "Natural" complies with FDA guidance (no artificial ingredients or added colors); functional claims support file compiled
- Allergen declaration accuracy: Cross-referenced to formulation; supplier allergen certifications obtained
- Manufacturer address and contact information; net quantity statement per FDA requirements (not net weight alone for liquids; must include fl. oz.)

**Claims Substantiation File:**
- Structure-function claims (e.g., "Supports healthy immune function"): Independent scientific literature supporting claim; at least one qualifying study (observational or clinical) demonstrating functional ingredient's efficacy. Cost: typically $500-2K for literature compilation and expert review
- Health claims (if applicable, e.g., "Reduces heart disease risk"): Requires "significant scientific agreement" per FDA; extremely limited for food products without pre-approval; escalate to Regulatory/Legal

**Output Documents:**
- Nutrition Facts Panel draft (PDF); final version incorporating lab results
- Ingredient statement with specification alignment
- Claims substantiation summary (1-page) referencing supporting literature
- Label mockup for final review approval

### Supply Chain Readiness & Approved Suppliers

Finalize supply chain for all critical ingredients and packaging:

**Approved Supplier List (ASL):**

| Material | Supplier Name | Contact | Lead Time | Min. Order | Capacity | Certification |
|----------|---------------|---------|-----------|-----------|----------|---------------|
| Ashwagandha Extract | Herbavate Inc. | sourcing@herbavate.com | 10 weeks | 25 kg | 500 kg/quarter | ISO 22000, Organic |
| Stevia (REB-A) | Ingredion Sweeteners | supplier@ingredion.com | 6 weeks | 100 kg | 1000 kg/quarter | FSSC 22000 |
| PET Bottle 12 oz | Consolidated Container Corp | sales@consolidated.com | 8 weeks | 50,000 units | 200,000/month | ISO 50001 |

**Purchase Agreements**: Execute supply agreements with lead suppliers specifying:
- Price (fixed or indexed commodity formula with price cap)
- Volume commitments (minimum annual purchase or capacity reservation; typical: Year 1 commitment 500K units for packaging)
- Delivery terms (FOB, lead times, tolerances for volume variance ±5%)
- Regulatory compliance (supplier certifies FSMA compliance, allergen controls, GRAS status of supplied ingredients)
- Quality: SLAs for on-time delivery, defect rate <0.5%, right of audit

**Safety Stock Plan:**
- Establish inventory buffer for critical, long-lead ingredients: typically 2-3 months' supply on hand
- Example: Ashwagandha extract with 10-week lead time and monthly consumption 100 kg → maintain 200-300 kg (2-3 month supply) to prevent stockouts
- Cost impact: Carrying cost ~1-2% of inventory value monthly; trade-off against supply chain risk

### Gate 3 Outputs: Production-Ready Product & Regulatory Clearances

Advance to Launch (Gate 4) only with:

1. **Validated Production Process**: Minimum 1 full-scale batch (ideally 3 consecutive) with Cpk ≥1.33; batch records and quality data documented; Process Authority approval obtained (if applicable)
2. **Quality Specifications**: Finished product and raw material specs approved by Quality and R&D
3. **Regulatory Clearances**: Nutrition Facts label verified by lab analysis; claims substantiation file complete; no outstanding regulatory issues
4. **Supply Chain Confirmed**: Approved supplier list finalized; purchase agreements signed; safety stock plan implemented; delivery confirmed for planned production schedule
5. **Financial Model Final**: COGS validated against actual production run costs; gross margin confirmed ≥50% (or recycle if margin erosion observed)
6. **Launch Readiness Plan**: Operations, Marketing, Sales, and Finance signatures confirming readiness for manufacturing and distribution

### Gate 3 Kill Criteria

Terminate projects if:

- **Production Yield <80%**: Indicates fundamental process flaw; cannot achieve acceptable COGS at commercial volumes
- **Process Authority Will Not Approve**: Thermal process validation fails safety criteria; risk of regulatory non-compliance unacceptable
- **COGS Actual >115% of Gate 1 Target**: Margin degradation from pilot to full-scale production exceeds tolerance; profit model unsustainable
- **Regulatory Clearance Cannot Be Obtained**: Unexpected regulatory barrier (ingredient GRAS petition denied, claims cannot be substantiated, state restriction identified) with no design-around solution within project timeline

### Gate 3 Recycle Criteria

Return to development if:

- **Yield 80-90%**: Requires process optimization (mixing speed/time, fill line calibration, material handling procedures); estimated 4-8 week improvement cycle; resubmit with optimized batch data
- **Minor Label Corrections Required**: Rounding adjustments to nutrient values, artwork fine-tuning, or minor claims language revision; <1 week to correct; re-gate after amendments

## Gate 4: Launch & Post-Launch

Gate 4 executes commercial launch and monitors early performance against plan. Not a stage per se, but a review checkpoint at Week 4, Week 8, and Week 12 post-launch to assess velocity and market reception.

### Commercialization Checklist

Pre-launch confirmation that all functions are ready:

**Retail & Distribution** (Sales function):
- [ ] Retail account confirmations: Secure purchase orders or distribution agreements from target accounts (by volume: Walmart, Target, key natural channels, DTC if applicable). Confirm sell-in dates, promotional support, planogram space.
- [ ] SKU activation: Bar codes assigned and registered with GS1; POS systems and inventory management systems updated; DSD (direct store delivery) routes planned if applicable
- [ ] Distribution set: Confirm shipments scheduled to distribution centers; delivery dates locked (typically 2-3 weeks prior to retail shelf date)

**Marketing & Promotions** (Marketing function):
- [ ] Brand messaging finalized: Positioning statement, claims messaging, consumer benefit articulation
- [ ] Trade promotion plan activated: Retailer incentives (slotting allowances, off-invoice discounts, sample programs) confirmed; timeline to execution (concurrent with distribution set)
- [ ] Consumer marketing assets ready: Digital assets (social media content calendar, paid media creative, website product pages), sampling programs, influencer partnerships if applicable
- [ ] PR & earned media: Press release distributed; media outreach to category reporters, food bloggers, nutritionists; target earned media launch window

**Operations & Manufacturing** (Operations/Quality):
- [ ] Production schedule locked: Release dates for manufacturing, packaging, QA testing, finished goods shipping confirmed; no conflicts with co-pack capacity or raw material availability
- [ ] Quality protocols active: Raw material lot testing, in-process checks, finished product release testing (all specs confirmed and documented)
- [ ] Shipping logistics planned: Carrier selection, lead times, distribution center delivery confirmation

**Finance** (Finance function):
- [ ] Pricing finalized: SRP locked, wholesale cost to accounts confirmed, margin projection updated
- [ ] Budget allocated: Year 1 marketing spend, manufacturing capacity reserved, working capital for inventory buildup confirmed
- [ ] Financial forecast: Sales velocity assumption, margin target, break-even timeline agreed

### Launch Execution

**Initial Production & Shipment:**
- Produce initial run (typically 5,000-20,000 units depending on market size and distribution breadth); fill quantities reflect 6-8 week retail sell-through projection
- Ship to distribution centers 2-3 weeks prior to planned retail shelf date (allows time for DCs to receive, QC, and replenish stores)
- Confirm delivery tracking; coordinate with retail partners on receipt and placement timing

**Retail Set Date:**
- Synchronized placement across prioritized accounts (ideally within 1-week window to maximize promotional impact and media coordination)
- Monitor initial in-stock at retail via syndicated data (Nielsen/IRI weekly reporting) or direct account visibility

### Post-Launch Review Cadence

**Week 4 Post-Launch Review:**
- **Velocity Check**: Actual units sold vs. Week 4 plan (proxy for full-week average, annualized projection)
- **Distribution Build**: % of targeted accounts in-stock; any out-of-stocks or stocking issues
- **Market Reception**: Retail feedback, consumer social sentiment, early repeat purchase signals if available
- **Marketing Effectiveness**: Digital engagement metrics, sampling program uptake, earned media impressions
- **Corrective Actions**: If velocity <75% of plan, identify barriers (distribution gaps, promotional ineffectiveness, product perception issues) and corrective actions

**Week 8 Post-Launch Review:**
- **Velocity Projection**: Annualized units at 8-week rate vs. plan; assess trajectory trend (accelerating, stable, declining)
- **Trial vs. Repeat**: Segment buyers into trial (single purchase) vs. repeat; repeat rate >25% indicates sustained consumer acceptance
- **Inventory Health**: Retailer inventory position (optimal 2-3 weeks supply); any excess or shortage signals
- **Marketing ROI**: Spending to date vs. incremental sales lift attribution

**Week 12 Post-Launch Review:**
- **Final Velocity Assessment**: Full quarterly performance vs. plan; decision: continue, expand, or consider discontinuation
- **Decision Framework**:
  - Velocity ≥80% of plan → Continue; plan Year 2 expansion (additional distribution, line extension, or new markets)
  - Velocity 50-80% of plan → Analyze root cause; execute corrective marketing, pricing, or promotional actions; re-review at Week 16
  - Velocity <50% of plan → Requires executive decision; path unclear unless distinct corrective opportunity identified

### Post-Launch Metrics & Monitoring

**Standard Metrics Tracked:**

- **Velocity (Units/Week)**: Actual sales divided by weeks in market; annualized to unit and revenue projections
- **Distribution (% ACV)**: All-Commodity Volume percentage: % of total retail outlets in target category carrying product; target ≥70% for mainstream launch, ≥40% for niche
- **Market Share (Volume & Revenue)**: % of category purchases attributable to SKU; benchmark against competitive set
- **Trial Rate**: % of category consumers who purchased product at least once in measured period (typically 8+ weeks); target ≥15-20% of target demographic
- **Repeat Rate**: % of trial consumers who repurchased within 12 weeks; target ≥20-30% indicates strong satisfaction (benchmark typically 15-25% for new food products)
- **Price/Promotion Elasticity**: Sales uplift during promotional periods vs. regular pricing; assess if product is price-elastic (high dependence on discounting) or sustained at full price
- **COGS Variance**: Actual COGS vs. Gate 3 model; flag any >5% variance for root cause analysis (ingredient cost volatility, yield deviation, packaging cost changes)

### Kill & Continue Criteria

**Post-Launch Kill Decision:**
- Velocity <50% of plan at Week 12 with no identified corrective pathway
- Margin erosion >10% vs. Gate 3 forecast, making profit contribution negative
- Retail returns/complaints spike above acceptable threshold (typically >2% of sales units returned or complaint rate >5 per 1M units)

**Continue/Expand Criteria:**
- Velocity ≥80% of plan; retail feedback positive; repeat rate ≥20%; margin acceptable
- Plan Year 2 actions: Add distribution, launch complementary SKU (flavor extension, format variation), expand to new retail channels (foodservice, e-commerce), geographic expansion

## Gate Review Meeting Structure

Effective gate reviews balance thorough evaluation with decision speed. Typical gate review cadence: 4-6 week intervals. Meeting lasts 2-3 hours with defined pre-work.

### Cross-Functional Attendance

Standard attendees (minimum critical mass; additional stakeholders invited as needed):

- **R&D Director/Head of Innovation**: Owns technical validation, formulation, shelf-life data
- **Marketing Director**: Owns market sizing, brand strategy, claims, consumer insights
- **Sales/Commercial Director**: Owns distribution strategy, retailer feedback, pricing
- **Operations/Manufacturing**: Owns COGS, supply chain, production capability, yield
- **Quality/Regulatory**: Owns safety compliance, regulatory status, label accuracy
- **Finance Controller or Business Manager**: Owns P&L assumptions, budget, working capital
- **Executive Sponsor** (VP level): Decision-maker in case of disagreement; ensures escalation path clarity

### Pre-Read Package Distribution

Distributed 3-5 business days prior to gate review meeting. Standard package contents:

1. **Executive Summary** (1 page): Project name, current stage, gate recommendation (Go/Kill/Recycle), key risk summary, decision required
2. **Stage Gate Scorecard** (1-2 pages): Current scores on go/kill/recycle criteria; comparison to prior stage thresholds
3. **Detailed Assessment Reports** (by function):
   - R&D: Formulation data, sensory results, shelf-life update, regulatory status
   - Marketing: Market sizing update, competitive positioning, consumer insights
   - Operations: COGS model (updated), supply chain status, manufacturing readiness
   - Quality/Regulatory: Compliance status, label review findings, approvals status
4. **Risk Register**: Known issues, open items, mitigation plans
5. **Timeline to Next Gate**: Milestones and resource requirements for advancement

### Decision Outcomes

Gate reviews generate four possible outcomes:

**1. GO** — Advance to next stage. Conditions (if any) documented; no contingencies require project rework.

**2. KILL** — Terminate project. Project formally archived; IP transferred to IP portfolio (if defensible); team reallocated.

**3. RECYCLE** — Return to current or prior stage with specific rework requirements. Gate review committee documents:
   - Specific deliverables required for re-gate (e.g., "Reformulate to achieve COGS <$0.75; re-test sensory; return to Gate 1 within 4 weeks")
   - Owner responsible for rework and re-gate scheduling
   - Revised timeline and budget impact

**4. HOLD** — Pause project pending external event or information (e.g., "Hold pending FDA response on ingredient petition; re-gate when response received"). Typical hold duration 4-12 weeks; project not terminated, but resources reallocated to active projects.

### Escalation Path for Disagreement

When gate review committee cannot reach consensus on Go/Kill/Recycle decision:

1. **Function-Level Debate** (first 30 minutes of decision discussion): Each function presents case for/against advancement; attempt consensus. Example disagreement: Operations says COGS unacceptable (>120% target), but Marketing argues strong consumer demand and proposes SKU as premium tier justifying higher COGS. Both positions documented.

2. **Executive Escalation**: If consensus not reached, escalate to Executive Sponsor (VP level) or Executive Innovation Committee. Committee evaluates strategic trade-offs (e.g., is portfolio gap in premium segment worth COGS risk?) and issues definitive decision (Go, Kill, or Recycle with specific conditions).

3. **Documentation**: Final decision documented in gate meeting minutes with rationale; tied to strategic portfolio objectives.

## Innovation Pipeline Management

Healthy innovation pipeline balances portfolio across multiple projects at various stages, ensuring consistent product flow and strategic coverage.

### Pipeline Funnel Metrics & Kill Rates

**Typical Portfolio Proportions** (inverted triangle):

| Stage | Target # Projects | % of Portfolio | Comments |
|-------|-------------------|----------------|----------|
| **Gate 0 (Ideation)** | 80-120 | 80-85% | High volume; ~15-20% kill rate (concept screening) |
| **Gate 1 (Feasibility)** | 12-20 | 10-15% | Fewer projects; 30-40% kill/recycle rate |
| **Gate 2 (Development)** | 4-8 | 4-6% | Significant investment; 20-30% kill/recycle rate |
| **Gate 3 (Validation)** | 2-4 | 2-3% | Late stage; <10% kill rate |
| **Gate 4 (Launch & beyond)** | 1-2 | 1-2% | Active products; post-launch reviews |

**Cumulative Kill Rate**: Typical ~90-95% of concepts submitted at Gate 0 never reach commercialization; this is healthy (filters weak ideas early). However, if cumulative kill rate <85% or >98%, pipeline screening rigor should be reassessed.

**Average Time Per Stage** (baseline, no external delays):

| Stage | Typical Duration | Range |
|-------|-----------------|-------|
| Gate 0 | 2-4 weeks | 1-6 weeks (rapid concept screening) |
| Gate 1 | 8-12 weeks | 6-16 weeks (depends on regulatory complexity) |
| Gate 2 | 16-24 weeks | 12-32 weeks (shelf-life extension, sensory cycles) |
| Gate 3 | 12-20 weeks | 8-28 weeks (depends on Process Authority timeline) |
| **Total to Launch** | **48-60 weeks** | **36-80 weeks** (typical 12-18 months) |

Regulatory delays (NDI notifications, food additive petitions) can extend timelines 6-12 months; front-load regulatory screening to minimize downstream delays.

### Pipeline Fill Rate Calculation

**Pipeline Fill Rate** = (Current projects in pipeline) / (Target portfolio capacity)

Example calculation:

- Target portfolio capacity: 15 projects in active pipeline (Gates 1-3)
- Current projects: Gate 1 (5), Gate 2 (6), Gate 3 (2) = 13 projects
- Fill rate: 13 / 15 = **87% (acceptable range 80-95%)**

If fill rate <75%, initiate accelerated concept screening (Gate 0) to replenish pipeline. If >95%, gate review committee should tighten go/kill criteria to prevent over-commitment of resources.

### Portfolio Balance: Core/Adjacent/Transformational

Balance projects across strategic horizons to manage risk and growth:

**Three-Horizon Portfolio Framework:**

1. **Core Innovation** (~70% of portfolio): Line extensions, flavor variants, or packaging format changes within existing category and distribution channel. Examples: New flavor of existing protein bar, single-serve version of existing RTD beverage. Margin protection, loyal customer retention. Time to market: 6-9 months.

2. **Adjacent Innovation** (~20% of portfolio): New category within broader consumer need or expanded distribution channel. Example: Company with protein bar portfolio launches protein powder (adjacent category). Same category consumers but different use occasion. Market expansion, new consumer segments. Time to market: 10-16 months.

3. **Transformational Innovation** (~10% of portfolio): Entry into fundamentally new category, new-to-company consumer segment, or disruptive business model. Example: Packaged foods company launches meal-kit delivery service. High risk, high reward; transforms company trajectory. Time to market: 18-24+ months; significant capital and capabilities investment.

**Portfolio Review Quarterly**: Confirm allocation across three horizons aligns with company strategy. If drift observed (e.g., 90% core, 8% adjacent, 2% transformational), adjust Gate 0 concept sourcing to rebalance.

### Resource Allocation Across Pipeline Stages

Resources (R&D budget, personnel, equipment time) allocated proportionally to stage investment required:

**Typical R&D Budget Allocation** (% of innovation budget):

- Gate 0 & 1: 20-25% (concept development, bench formulation, preliminary feasibility work)
- Gate 2: 40-50% (pilot trials, shelf-life studies, consumer testing, artwork development)
- Gate 3: 25-30% (production trials, final validation, regulatory clearances)
- Post-Launch: 5-10% (optimization, troubleshooting, line extension planning)

If actual resource allocation deviates >10% from planned allocation, productivity metrics decline (longer stage duration, gate cycle time extends beyond baseline). Rebalance quarterly or address bottleneck (e.g., if Gate 2 extending beyond 24 weeks, increase sensory lab capacity or pilot manufacturing resources).

## Final Notes on Implementation

The stage-gate process reduces innovation risk through disciplined screening, but does not eliminate market risk entirely. Post-launch velocity monitoring (Weeks 4, 8, 12) provides early warning of demand shortfall and enables pivot decisions. Project teams must balance process rigor with speed-to-market; overly stringent gating delays entry to market and risks trend obsolescence, while insufficient rigor leads to low-quality launches and failed commercialization.

Regular pipeline audits (quarterly minimum) ensure process health, identify systemic bottlenecks (e.g., persistent Gate 2 delays due to shelf-life study timelines), and enable resource reallocation to highest-priority projects.
